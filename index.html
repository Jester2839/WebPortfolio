What am I...

Beginnings and studies

I am a simple boy from the suburbs of Pardubice who fell in love with coding in elementary school and photography in high school, and then relentlessly pursued his dream. I struggled through elementary school, bringing home bad grades every week and having my parents constantly called by the school about what I had done. Then I started practicing martial arts and art, and finally began to behave myself. Then I transferred to a math school, where I discovered what I was capable of. That's where I started to enjoy programming and digging into code. So when I found out about Delta in 8th grade, it was clear where I would go next. I did everything I could to get there... and it paid off. When I finally enrolled, my "years at Delta" began. It was only here that I discovered my true potential and began to devote myself fully to what I enjoy and find fulfilling.

Travels and photographs

Ever since we were little, our parents took us on trips whenever they could. Since we are a climbing family, it was always somewhere in the mountains, among the rocks... and that's how it stayed with me. Going on vacation to a resort is not an option for us; we prefer to go climbing, ski mountaineering, or just hiking in the mountains a few times a year, because it's just more fun. I started escaping to the mountains as soon as I could. At 16, I spontaneously went on a three-day hike in the mountains, and I didn't sleep in hotels... I carried 10 kg on my back and slept in the wild, because otherwise it's just not the same. And that connected beautifully with my new hobby, photography. At the end of my freshman year, we went to Litomyšl for a week to learn photography. And there I found exactly what I was missing, some kind of creative activity, and that for me is photography. I knew that I had always had an eye for detail and at least some creative spirit, but it was only when I started taking pictures that I really felt it. When people started praising my photos, I knew that I would enjoy this. I started with a discarded, 20-year-old camera from my grandmother. It wasn't anything special, but it produced some nice photos and was the perfect machine to start with. I dare say that even after all these years, it's still a competitive machine. After all, everything you see on this website was taken with it. I'm not saying I'm good at it, but I think and believe that I have some kind of future in it, at least as an amateur.

Programming and website development

As I said, I got into coding in elementary school. It's not that I excelled at it, but I quite enjoyed it and didn't have any other ideas of what to focus on. So I joined Delta, and they brought out this talent, or whatever you want to call it, in me. What really helps me with programming is that I have an eye for detail and I'm a huge perfectionist (which is usually more of a problem than an advantage). So when I get into something, I give it my all and no matter what it takes, I just get it to my imaginary perfect end. When I started learning how to create websites in my second year, I was finally able to combine logic with creativity and make it one. So, by combining all my strengths and weaknesses, I always went overboard. Everyone around me did the bare minimum, but I... I always had it all figured out and brought it to my megalomaniacal conclusion. The web is one of the few things that began to fulfill me, and let's face it, it's not a bad way to earn some extra money while studying. I'm just starting out, but I believe and hope that over time I'll get to a level where I can compete with the best in the field.
